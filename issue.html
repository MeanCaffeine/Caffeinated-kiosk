    Roads

    Roads are critical for any city to have in order for people and things to move around the city. While most city streets can legally be used by cars, trucks, motorcycles, machinery, and bikes, you will quickly find that the road is not equal-oppurtunity. Those with cars have the most space, with bicycles having the smallest. For traveling long distance, this might make sense- but most journeys within the city are only a mile or two. The space that every car and truck occupy is many times what an individual would normally take up. This leads to traffic jams, slow and inneficient movement, hazardous smog and pollution, and unpleasant noise for all.

    Sidewalks & Stairs

    Pittsburgh has many sidwalks and many sets of public stairs. In a time before personal vehicles, these stairs served as the earliest form of public transportation: getting workers from their hilltop homes to the factories and mines down below. However, if you hope to traverse the city, they can often be frustrating. You'll quickly find that as a pedestrian, you're treated as a second-rate citizen. Waiting for crossing lights, waving down cars for the privlege of crossing, and dodging turning vehicles when they turn on red. Our thoroughfares are hostile to humans.

    Public Transport

    When walking is not an option, Public Transit is a wonderful space and energy efficient way to get around. Pittsburgh has busways to make this travel quicker.. but not quick enough. Unlike other cities, Pittsburgh has no bus-only lanes. This means they are hamstrung by traffic just like cars. Slower service means more people choose to drive out of necesity or convenience. To make matters worse, Pittsburgh has the shortest urban light rail network in the country, making it almost useless as is. Be honest, did you even remember Pittsburgh had a light rail network?
